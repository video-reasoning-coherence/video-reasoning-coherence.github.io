Reasoning Coherence

Example: Apple Falling — Physics-based Reasoning

Prompt: Generate the motion that an apple falls straight down.

Veo-3
Cosmos
Kling
Reasoning Coherence is the extent to which generated video events maintain consistent and plausible cause-and-effect relationships over time — spanning long-horizon dependencies, interaction reasoning, and the fidelity of intermediate transitions. In other words, it's not about whether the video looks good, but whether what happens in the video is causally consistent across frames.

No Hint, Visual Hint & Text Hint

We explore three prompting strategies for video reasoning: No Hint (plain task), Visual Hint (annotated image guidance), and Text Hint (answer-aware prompt). Slide through examples to see how hints affect model outputs.

Example 1 — Physics: Block on Rough Semicircular Track

No Hint
No Hint Prompt
Show the rough semicircular track with a block at point P; release it and let it slide naturally. Static shot.
Visual Hint
Visual Hint Prompt
Show the rough semicircular track with height label h. An annotated arrow shows friction direction. Release the block from P and show it stops below the rim on the other side. Static shot.
Text Hint
Text Hint Prompt
The answer is: the block rises to a height less than h due to energy loss from friction. Animate the block sliding down and rising to a clearly lower height on the opposite side. Static shot.

Takeaways

💡 Takeaway 1: Reasoning Coherence vs. Generation Quality

High generation quality does not guarantee reasoning coherence. Some models (e.g., Kling) produce visually stunning videos but fail to capture the correct reasoning. Conversely, lower-fidelity outputs can still answer the question correctly.

Kling — Beautiful but Wrong

Q: Which numbered brick will the ball hit first?

Gen Quality: 3.8/4 Reasoning: 0.5/4

Visually smooth and high-fidelity, but the ball trajectory violates reflection physics.

Veo-3 — Rough but Correct

Q: Which numbered brick will the ball hit first?

Gen Quality: 1.8/4 Reasoning: 3.2/4

Lower visual fidelity, but the ball correctly follows reflection rules.

📝 Takeaway 2: Text Hint — Double-Edged Sword

Text hints with answer information can boost reasoning coherence in many cases, but may also introduce hallucinations when the model over-relies on the hint and generates content inconsistent with the visual input.

Text Hint Helps ✓

Without hint: Model counts 5 squares (wrong)

With text hint: "The line passes through 8 squares." → Model correctly highlights 8.

No Hint: 1.0/4 Text Hint: 3.5/4
Text Hint Hallucination ✗

Without hint: Model correctly zooms to row (score 2.5)

With text hint: Model fabricates data values from the hint, distorting the chart.

No Hint: 2.5/4 Text Hint: 0.8/4

👁️ Takeaway 3: Visual Hint — Grounding Helps, Clutter Hurts

Visual hints (bounding boxes, arrows, highlighted regions) generally improve spatial grounding, but in cluttered scenes they can confuse the model or shift attention away from the reasoning target.

Visual Hint Helps ✓

Without hint: Model pans to wrong player

With visual hint: Arrow from #10 → basketball. Model correctly identifies spatial relation.

No Hint: 1.2/4 Visual Hint: 3.0/4
Visual Hint Distraction ✗

Without hint: Model correctly identifies motorcycle position

With visual hint: Overlapping bounding boxes in the cluttered scene mislead the model.

No Hint: 2.8/4 Visual Hint: 1.3/4
